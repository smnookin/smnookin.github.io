"Stanley Kubrick's films were landmark events - majestic, memorable and richly researched. But, as the years went by, the time between films grew longer and longer, and less and less was seen of the director. <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/film/2004/mar/27/features.weekend">What on earth was he doing?</a>" In a moving, humorous essay in _The Guardian_, Ronson, the author of _Men Who Stare at Goats_, attempts to answer that question.
