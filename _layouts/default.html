One of a Kind

What do you if your child has a condition that's new to science? In 2012, Matt and Cristina Might learned that their son, Bertrand, was the first person in the world with NGLY1 disorder. In the June 21, 2014 issue of The New Yorker, Seth Mnookin looked at how social media is changing the hunt for rare diseases -- and the conflicts between researchers and their human subjects.

Fear and Loathing on the Campaign Trail '72

After the success of Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas, his 1972 masterpiece, Hunter S. Thompson set out to cover the '72 campaign for Rolling Stone. These articles, which are among the best political journalism ever and the finest of Thompson's work, were eventually collected in a best-selling book.

The final comeback of Axl Rose

In the fall of 2006, four years after disappearing from public view, a long John Jeremiah Sullivan piece in GQ grappled with the legacy of the greatest--or is it the wierdest?--frontman of all time during a period when Rose was biting the legs of security guards and gyrating, shrieking, and storming off stages around the world.
